A production-grade cut,
from a conversation.

Editron isn't a UI wrapped around ffmpeg. It's a reasoning loop that treats audio as primary, visuals as secondary, and taste as the thing you negotiate together.

◐

Word-level transcripts

ElevenLabs Scribe with speaker diarization and audio events. Every cut snaps to a word boundary — no mid-syllable clips.

◑

Strategy before cuts

Editron reads the material, proposes a shape in plain English, and waits for confirmation. No edits before you agree.

◒

Per-segment grading

Lossless concat. 30ms audio fades. Overlays PTS-shifted. Subtitles last. The twelve hard rules of production-correct video, encoded.

◓

Parallel animations

PIL, Manim, or Remotion — spawned as parallel sub-agents. Total wall time is the slowest one, not the sum.

◔

Social-ready loudness

Two-pass EBU R128: -14 LUFS, -1 dBTP, LRA 11. Ships at the level every platform wants.

◕

Session memory

Every session appends to project.md. Next week's cut picks up where you left off — no context reload.

The loop

Ask → confirm → execute → iterate.

  1. 01

    Drop footage

    Any folder with .mp4, .mov, or .mkv. Editron walks the tree, probes every file, transcribes in parallel.

  2. 02

    Describe what you want

    "Edit these into a launch video." "Tighten this tutorial." "Make it punchy." Editron asks the right questions — shaped by what it sees, not from a fixed form.

  3. 03

    Approve the plan

    Four to eight sentences: shape, takes, cuts, grade, animations, subtitles, length. You confirm in plain English.

  4. 04

    Preview & iterate

    A 720p preview drops in your edit folder. Natural-language feedback reshapes the cut. Final render on the word ship.
